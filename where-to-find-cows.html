Where to Find Cow Breeds

Holstein: Originally from the Netherlands, Holsteins are now the most common dairy cow breed in the U.S. You’ll find them on large dairy farms across Wisconsin, California, and New York.

Jersey: These smaller cows hail from the British Channel Island of Jersey. Today, they’re popular on smaller dairy farms in states like Vermont and Pennsylvania for their rich milk.

Angus: A beef breed from Scotland, Angus cows are widely raised in Texas, Kansas, and Oklahoma. They’re known for their marbled meat and are a favorite in American ranching.

Hereford: Originally from England, Herefords are popular throughout the American Midwest. You’ll often see them in ranches across South Dakota, Nebraska, and Montana.

Guernsey: Another Channel Island breed, Guernseys are more rare but can be found on specialty dairy farms in the Northeast and parts of the Pacific Northwest.
